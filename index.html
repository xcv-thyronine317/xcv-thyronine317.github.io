💾 NAS Storage Guide

A NAS (Network Attached Storage) is a dedicated storage system connected to your local network. It allows computers, phones, and other devices to store and access files from one central location.

🏠 Home NAS

Typical capacity:
4TB - 40TB

  • Family backups
  • Personal cloud
  • Movies and music
  • Photo libraries

⚙️ Advanced NAS

Typical capacity:
40TB - 200TB+

  • Large media libraries
  • Multiple users
  • Small businesses

🏢 Enterprise NAS

Capacity:
Hundreds of TB to Petabytes

  • Companies
  • Research centers
  • Large archives

RAID Protection Explained

RAID Type Purpose
RAID 0 Maximum speed but no protection.
RAID 1 Mirrors data across drives.
RAID 5 Balance between capacity, speed, and protection.
RAID 6 Protection against multiple drive failures.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the fastest consumer storage?

High-end NVMe SSDs currently provide some of the fastest consumer storage speeds, reaching several thousand MB/s.

What storage lasts the longest?

Current available archival technologies include M-DISC and magnetic tape. Future technologies like DNA and crystal storage may last much longer but are not commercially available.

Is NAS better than cloud storage?

NAS provides local control and ownership. Cloud storage provides easier remote access and off-site redundancy.

How much storage could future technology hold?

DNA, atomic, molecular, and crystal storage could potentially exceed current storage densities by enormous amounts.

How much information can the human brain store?

Some estimates compare the brain to petabyte-scale storage, although biological memory works differently from digital storage.
