💾 NAS Storage Guide

A NAS (Network Attached Storage) is a dedicated storage system connected to your network. It allows multiple devices to access files, backups, media libraries, and applications from one location.

🏠 Home NAS

Typical capacity:
4TB - 40TB

Good for:

  • Family photo backups
  • Movies and music servers
  • Personal cloud replacement
  • Game backups

⚙️ Advanced NAS

Typical capacity:
40TB - 200TB+

Good for:

  • Large media libraries
  • Multiple users
  • Small businesses

🏢 Enterprise NAS

Capacity:
Hundreds of TB to Petabytes

Used by:

  • Companies
  • Research facilities
  • Large archives

RAID Protection Explained

RAID Type Purpose
RAID 0 Combines drives for speed but no protection.
RAID 1 Mirrors data on two drives for redundancy.
RAID 5 Balances capacity, speed, and protection.
RAID 6 Protects against multiple drive failures.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the fastest consumer storage?

High-end NVMe SSDs are currently among the fastest consumer storage technologies, reaching several thousand MB/s.

What storage lasts the longest?

Available technologies such as M-DISC and archival tape are designed for long-term preservation. Experimental technologies such as DNA and quartz crystal storage may last much longer but are not widely available.

Is NAS better than cloud storage?

NAS gives ownership and control of your hardware. Cloud storage provides easier remote access and off-site protection.

How much data can humanity store in the future?

Future technologies such as DNA, atomic, and molecular storage could greatly increase storage density compared with current drives.

How much storage does the human brain have?

Some estimates compare the brain to petabyte-scale information capacity, although biological memory is not directly comparable to computer storage.
