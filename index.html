NAS Storage Explained

NAS (Network Attached Storage) is a dedicated storage device connected to a network. It allows multiple computers, phones, and media devices to access the same files.

🏠 Home NAS

Typical capacity:
4TB - 40TB

Used for:
Photos, videos, backups, personal media servers

⚙️ Advanced NAS

Typical capacity:
40TB - 200TB+

Used for:
Power users and small businesses

🏢 Enterprise NAS

Capacity:
Hundreds of TB to Petabytes

Used by:
Companies and data centers

Common RAID Configurations

RAID Purpose
RAID 0 Maximum speed but no protection
RAID 1 Mirrors data for safety
RAID 5 Balance of capacity and protection
RAID 6 Extra protection for large arrays

Storage Units Explained

Digital storage increases by thousands at each level.

Unit Approximate Size Example
KB (Kilobyte) 1,000 bytes Small text files
MB (Megabyte) 1,000 KB Photos, songs, ebooks
GB (Gigabyte) 1,000 MB Phones, games, movies
TB (Terabyte) 1,000 GB Hard drives and media collections
PB (Petabyte) 1,000 TB Large servers
