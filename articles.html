⚡ SSD vs HDD

Solid-state drives use flash memory and provide much faster access speeds than traditional hard drives.

Hard disk drives remain useful because they provide large capacities at lower cost, especially for backups and archives.

🎮 Console Storage Evolution

Storage requirements have increased dramatically over generations.

  • Retro systems: MB to GB
  • PS2/GameCube/Xbox era: hundreds of MB per game
  • PS3/Xbox 360 era: several GB per game
  • Modern consoles: tens to hundreds of GB per game

💾 Understanding NAS Storage

A NAS allows multiple devices to access shared storage through a network.

Common uses include backups, personal cloud storage, media servers, and file sharing.

📀 Long-Term Data Preservation

No single storage technology is perfect forever. Professional preservation usually combines multiple copies, different locations, and regular migration.

🧬 DNA Data Storage

DNA storage is an experimental technology that converts digital information into biological molecules.

Its main advantage is extreme theoretical density, but current systems are slow and expensive.

🪟 5D Crystal Storage

Quartz crystal storage uses lasers to create microscopic structures capable of representing information.

Research suggests extremely long stability, but it is not currently a consumer storage product.

⚛ Atomic and Molecular Storage

Researchers are investigating whether individual atoms or molecules could store information.

These technologies could greatly increase future storage density.
